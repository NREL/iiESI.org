Educational Opportunities

The International Institute for Energy Systems Integration strives to establish a common understanding of the very complex area of energy systems integration through internships and educational opportunities.

Courses

Upcoming courses include:

Energy Systems Integration 101, July 21–25, 2014

The National Renewable Energy Laboratory, Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, Electricity Research Centre of University College Dublin, and Danish Technical University present an introductory course on energy systems integration. The course will cover the concept of energy systems integration at all scales (residential to continental) across multiple energy (and non-energy) domains (e.g., electricity and gas nexus, electricity and water nexus, gas and transport) from technical, market, and regulatory perspectives. The course is designed to give PhD students and practitioners working in the field a perspective on the state of the art in energy systems integration. Each of the first four days will consist of four hours of lecture and four hours of project work. The final day will be for each team to report out on its project.

Internships

Over the summer of 2014, the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) will run a pilot international internship program for graduate students pursuing careers related to power systems engineering. The formal 8–10 week internship program will commence with the weeklong energy systems integration intensive and culminate with a final project presentation. After the energy systems integration intensive, NREL will facilitate matching students with NREL researchers through a series of presentations at an energy systems integration fair designed to generate student interest in working on specific research projects. Once students indicate interest, NREL researchers will interview interested interns so mutual matches are formed.
