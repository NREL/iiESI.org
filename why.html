Why iiESI?

Today's energy systems—those that provide us with heat, water, electricity, and transportation—must evolve to address the critical challenges associated with the rising cost of energy, the effects of climate change, and the resulting threats to our energy security.

Tackling these issues requires us to change the way we think about energy, to share the knowledge we gain, and to evolve our current energy systems.

That's why we established the International Institute for Energy Systems Integration (iiESI), a global institute aimed at solving energy challenges through collaboration and education on energy systems integration. These coordinated efforts will ensure that worldwide R&D investments are optimized to yield the greatest value possible to the global community.

Learn more about who we are and how to join us.

What Is Energy Systems Integration?

Energy systems integration (ESI) is a holistic view of all energy systems we use today—not just electrical, but also transportation, heat, and water—to create an integrated energy system that leverages the strengths of each system within it. This harmonization will result in efficiency gains in both energy and cost.

ESI is about tapping into the combined strength of energy systems—from those that operate in individual homes and communities to those that span whole regions of the world—and maximizing the value of every unit of energy we use. By focusing on the optimization of energy systems across multiple pathways and scales, we can better understand how to increase reliability, reduce cost, and minimize environmental impacts of our energy systems.
